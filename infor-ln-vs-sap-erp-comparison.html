Platform Overview — Who Are They?

Both Infor LN and SAP S/4HANA are mature, enterprise-grade ERP platforms with decades of real-world implementation history. But they were built for different markets and carry different philosophies.

Infor LN (formerly Baan ERP) has been a trusted name in discrete manufacturing since the 1990s. Now part of Infor — a Koch Industries-backed enterprise software company — LN is purpose-built for complex manufacturing environments. It powers global operations in aerospace, automotive, industrial equipment, and high-tech sectors. With the cloud-native Infor CloudSuite Industrial, LN has transitioned into a modern, multi-tenant platform while retaining its deep manufacturing DNA.

SAP S/4HANA is the next-generation ERP from SAP SE, the world's largest enterprise application company. It replaces the legacy SAP ECC platform and runs on SAP's in-memory HANA database. SAP's scope is vast — it serves virtually every industry and enterprise function, from finance and HR to supply chain, procurement, and manufacturing. SAP commands a dominant share of the Fortune 500 ERP market.

Key distinction: Infor LN was designed from the ground up for manufacturing. SAP was designed as a broad enterprise platform that spans every industry and function. This foundational difference shapes everything — from out-of-the-box functionality to implementation approach.

Manufacturing and Supply Chain Capabilities

Manufacturing is where the difference between these platforms is felt most acutely. Both are capable, but they approach the problem from different angles.

Infor LN — Manufacturing First

Infor LN was built for complex, multi-level manufacturing from day one. Its core modules cover production planning, shop-floor control, engineering change management, project-based manufacturing, and advanced planning and scheduling (APS). For companies that run engineer-to-order (ETO) or configure-to-order (CTO) operations, LN's out-of-the-box support is remarkably deep — often requiring less customisation than competing platforms to handle complex bill-of-material structures, work-breakdown schedules, and milestone billing.

SAP S/4HANA — Breadth and Integration

SAP's manufacturing capabilities are comprehensive and cover discrete, process, lean, and repetitive manufacturing models. Where SAP excels is in integrating manufacturing tightly with finance, procurement, warehousing, quality management, and human capital management — all on a single real-time data platform powered by HANA. For large conglomerates with both manufacturing and non-manufacturing divisions, SAP's ability to provide a single source of truth across the entire enterprise is a powerful differentiator.

Practical takeaway: If your business is predominantly a complex discrete manufacturer and that is the core of what you do, Infor LN will likely feel more natural and require less configuration. If manufacturing is one part of a diverse business portfolio, SAP's breadth becomes a strategic advantage.

Total Cost of Ownership

Cost is rarely the only factor, but it is always a significant one. The total cost of ownership for an ERP goes well beyond licence fees.

Cost Component Infor LN SAP S/4HANA
Licence / Subscription Moderate — competitive per-user pricing Higher — premium pricing reflects broad scope
Implementation Cost Lower — manufacturing-first design reduces configuration effort for discrete manufacturers Higher — greater complexity, more consultants, longer timelines for comparable scope
Consultant Availability Smaller talent pool — can increase rates Large global talent pool — more competitive rates at volume
Annual Maintenance / Cloud Fees Standard (typically 18–22% of licence or subscription-based) Standard but higher absolute cost given larger licence base
Typical 5-Year TCO Range $1M – $8M (mid-market manufacturer) $3M – $25M+ (depending on scope and enterprise size)

Executive insight: For a mid-market discrete manufacturer, Infor LN typically delivers a 30–50% lower total cost of ownership compared to SAP S/4HANA for equivalent manufacturing functionality. However, if your organisation requires broad coverage across HR, CRM, procurement, and analytics from a single vendor, SAP's all-in cost may be justifiable.

Implementation complexity is one of the most significant differentiators. Infor LN's manufacturing-first architecture means that many common manufacturing scenarios are supported out of the box, reducing the need for extensive configuration or customisation. SAP projects tend to be larger in scope and require more rigorous programme management — but they also deliver a broader enterprise platform at the end.

Strengths and Limitations

Infor LN Strengths

  • Purpose-built for complex discrete manufacturing
  • Strong multi-site, multi-company capabilities
  • Lower total cost of ownership for manufacturing
  • Faster implementation timelines
  • Deep ETO / CTO / project manufacturing support
  • Modern cloud option via CloudSuite Industrial

Areas for Improvement

  • Smaller partner and consultant ecosystem
  • HR and CRM modules less mature
  • Reporting tools evolving but not best-in-class
  • Lower global brand recognition outside manufacturing
VS

SAP S/4HANA Strengths

  • Broadest functional coverage across all business areas
  • Real-time analytics on HANA in-memory database
  • Massive global partner and talent ecosystem
  • Strong finance, HR (SuccessFactors), and procurement
  • Industry solutions for nearly every vertical
  • Leading AI and automation investment (SAP Business AI)

Areas for Improvement

  • Higher total cost of ownership
  • Longer, more complex implementations
  • Forced HANA database requirement
  • Legacy complexity can slow agility

Decision Framework — Which ERP Fits You?

There is no universally "better" ERP. The right choice depends on your business context. Use the profiles below to identify which platform aligns with your organisation.

Choose Infor LN If...

Your business is primarily a discrete manufacturer — especially if you operate in aerospace, defence, automotive, industrial equipment, or high-tech. You run engineer-to-order or project-based manufacturing. You need a faster time-to-value with a focused implementation. Your IT budget favours a lower total cost of ownership. You want deep manufacturing functionality out of the box without building a sprawling enterprise platform.

Choose SAP S/4HANA If...

Your organisation operates across multiple industries or has significant non-manufacturing business units. You need a single integrated platform for finance, HR, procurement, CRM, and manufacturing. You have the budget and programme management capacity for a larger-scale implementation. Real-time analytics and AI capabilities are strategic priorities. Your board and stakeholders value the global brand recognition and large partner ecosystem that SAP provides.

Final thought: The best ERP is the one that fits your business processes, your budget, and your team's ability to adopt it. Whichever platform you choose, success depends less on the software and more on the quality of your implementation partner, your change management strategy, and the training investment you make in your people.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Infor LN only for manufacturing companies?

Infor LN is heavily optimised for discrete manufacturing, but it includes full financials, procurement, warehousing, and service management modules. That said, if manufacturing is not your core business, SAP or other generalist ERPs may be a better fit.

Can I migrate from SAP to Infor LN (or vice versa)?

Yes, migrations in both directions happen regularly. Moving from SAP to Infor LN is common among mid-market manufacturers who find SAP's complexity and cost disproportionate to their needs. Moving from Infor LN to SAP typically happens when a company is acquired by a larger SAP-standardised group. Both scenarios require careful data migration planning and a qualified implementation partner.

How does Infor's acquisition by Koch Industries (and IBM's involvement) affect LN's future?

Koch Industries acquired Infor in 2020, and IBM became a strategic partner via the Infor consulting arm. Koch's backing provides long-term financial stability and a commitment to continued R&D investment. Infor LN is actively developed, with regular cloud updates and new AI features via the Infor OS platform.

What about SAP's mandatory move to S/4HANA?

SAP has set a mainstream maintenance end date of 2027 for ECC, with extended maintenance available until 2030. This is driving a large wave of S/4HANA migrations. If you are currently on SAP ECC, this is a natural decision point to also evaluate whether Infor LN might be a better long-term fit for your manufacturing operations.

Which ERP has better support for global, multi-language, multi-currency operations?

Both platforms handle multi-language and multi-currency operations well. Infor LN has native multi-company and inter-company trading capabilities that are particularly strong for manufacturing groups with complex intercompany transactions. SAP has broader localisation coverage across more countries, which matters for organisations with diverse regulatory requirements.
