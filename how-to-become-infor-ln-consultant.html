Why Infor LN? The ERP That Runs Complex Manufacturing

Infor LN is the enterprise ERP of choice for discrete, process, and mixed-mode manufacturers worldwide — and skilled consultants who can implement, configure, and support it are in constant demand.

Unlike generic ERP platforms, Infor LN (formerly Baan) is purpose-built for manufacturing and supply chain operations. It handles everything from shop-floor production orders and multi-site planning to global financials and regulatory compliance — making the consultants who master it highly valuable and well-compensated.

Whether you are a fresh graduate exploring an ERP career, an existing functional consultant looking to specialise, or a technical professional wanting to cross into consulting, this guide maps the entire journey for you.

The Infor LN Consultant Career Path

Most practitioners move through four progressive stages. The timeline varies based on prior ERP experience, training quality, and the modules you specialise in.

01

Junior Functional / Technical Consultant

0–2 Years Experience

You learn Infor LN navigation, master one or two core modules (e.g., Manufacturing or Financials), support senior consultants during implementations, and handle basic configuration tasks and user training.

02

Functional Consultant – Module Specialist

2–5 Years Experience

You lead module-level workstreams independently. You conduct fit-gap analyses, design solutions, configure production orders, procurement flows, or financial periods end-to-end, and mentor junior team members.

03

Senior Consultant / Solution Architect

5–10 Years Experience

You own full project streams or act as the primary client liaison. You design cross-module integrations, lead blueprint workshops, translate complex business requirements into LN configurations, and guide technical teams on customisations.

04

Principal Consultant / Practice Lead

10+ Years Experience

You drive the overall delivery strategy for large or multi-site implementations, build consulting practices, define methodology standards, and are often involved in pre-sales, bid responses, and executive-level client engagement.

Functional vs. Technical: Which Path Is Right for You?

Functional consultants focus on business processes — understanding how a manufacturing operation works and configuring LN to match it. A background in supply chain, finance, or operations is a natural entry point.

Technical consultants focus on customisation, integration (4GL / ION), data migration, and reporting. A background in programming, databases, or IT infrastructure suits this path.

Many experienced practitioners develop skills in both areas, commanding the highest rates as cross-functional solution architects.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a manufacturing background to become an Infor LN consultant?

Not necessarily, but it helps for functional roles. Many successful consultants come from finance, supply chain, or IT backgrounds. What matters more is your ability to learn business processes and map them to system configurations. FullOnBaan's courses teach both the LN system and the underlying business logic.

How long does it take to become job-ready as an Infor LN consultant?

With dedicated, structured training, most motivated learners become job-ready in 3–6 months for a junior role. Completing a certification alongside your course significantly strengthens your candidacy and can shorten this timeline by improving your confidence and credibility.

Is Infor LN still relevant with cloud ERP growing?

Yes. Infor LN is actively invested in by Infor (an IBM company since 2024), with a cloud deployment option (Infor CloudSuite Industrial). Thousands of manufacturers globally run LN and have long upgrade cycles, ensuring consulting demand for many years ahead.

Can I work remotely as an Infor LN consultant?

Increasingly yes. Post-pandemic, many LN implementations are conducted remotely for the majority of the project, with on-site visits for workshops and go-live support. Senior consultants often work fully remote or in hybrid models, especially for support and managed services roles.

Which modules should I learn first?

Manufacturing (Production Management) and Finance (Financials) are the most in-demand across all implementations. Starting with either of these gives you the widest range of initial job opportunities. FullOnBaan's courses are structured to guide you through these modules step by step.
